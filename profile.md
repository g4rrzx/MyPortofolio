# Role
Act as an expert UI/UX Designer and Frontend Developer specializing in personal portfolio websites for tech professionals.

# Objective
Create a modern, responsive, and high-performance personal portfolio website. The design should be minimalist yet futuristic, suitable for a developer/researcher with a focus on Web3 technologies.

# Design Aesthetic & Theme
- **Color Palette:** Dark mode by default. Use deep charcoal or black backgrounds (#0a0a0a) with accent colors like electric blue, purple, or neon green to highlight Web3 elements.
- **Typography:** Clean sans-serif fonts (e.g., Inter, Roboto, or Space Grotesk) for a technical look.
- **Visual Style:** Glassmorphism effects for cards, subtle gradients, and smooth micro-interactions on hover.
- **Layout:** Single-page application (SPA) style with smooth scroll navigation, or a clean multi-section landing page.

# Site Structure & Content Requirements

## 1. Hero Section (Header)
- **Headline:** Large, bold text introducing the name and role (e.g., "Building the Decentralized Future").
- **Subheadline:** A brief one-sentence pitch about who I am (e.g., "Developer, Researcher, and Web3 Enthusiast").
- **CTA Buttons:** Two buttons: "View My Work" (primary) and "Contact Me" (secondary/outline).
- **Visual:** A subtle animated background or 3D element that represents connectivity or blockchain nodes.

## 2. About Me Section
- **Content:** A short bio paragraph describing my background, passion for technology, and current focus.
- **Skills:** A visual tag cloud or grid displaying technical skills (e.g., Solidity, React, Python, Smart Contracts, UI/UX).
- **Info:** Include location, availability status, and education background briefly.

## 3. General Projects Section
- **Layout:** A responsive grid of project cards.
- **Card Details:** Each card must include:
  - Project Title.
  - Short description.
  - Tech stack used (icons or tags).
  - Links: "Live Demo" and "GitHub Repository".
  - Hover effect: Slight lift and glow.

## 4. Web3 Research Projects (Featured Section)
- **Distinction:** This section should look distinct from the general projects (e.g., a different background shade or a bordered section) to emphasize its importance.
- **Layout:** List or detailed card view.
- **Content:** Focus on research papers, whitepapers, or experimental dApps.
- **Card Details:**
  - Research Title.
  - Abstract/Summary of the research.
  - Tags (e.g., "DeFi", "ZK-Proofs", "Governance").
  - Link to "Read Paper" or "View Case Study".

## 5. Contact Section
- **Form:** A clean contact form (Name, Email, Message).
- **Direct Info:** Display email address clearly.
- **Social Links:** Prominent icons for LinkedIn, GitHub, Twitter/X, and Discord/Telegram (crucial for Web3).
- **Footer:** Copyright info and simple links to privacy policy if needed.

# Technical Requirements
- **Responsiveness:** Must look perfect on Mobile, Tablet, and Desktop.
- **Performance:** Optimize for fast loading (lazy load images).
- **Accessibility:** Ensure high contrast and proper ARIA labels.
- **Framework:** If generating code, use React, Tailwind CSS, and Framer Motion for animations.

# Tone of Voice
Professional, innovative, and approachable.
